The Society of Visual Language

Editorial

Research

2018

‘The Society of Visual Language’ is a part of a graduation dissertation (2018). It aims to rethink the role of images and the interaction between the audiences and the creators. We use images as a method of communication to deliver messages to the audiences as the same way as we would using verbal communication, however, it is operated using a different process from other communication forms.

‘The Society of Visual Language’ is a physical publication that represents theoretical and visual research into signs and their meaning, and how they work in images as a visual language. The publication is based on the theoretical research of Roland Barthes and Ferdinand de Saussure. By adding a few examples of advertising campaigns which use images to create value of a brand, ‘The Society of Visual Language’ describes a relationship between a culture of consumption and visual language.
