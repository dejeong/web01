A Shape of Emotions

Experimental design

Creative direction

2018

The world we are living in is full of data and system, and they work with algorithms. Data is based on mathematics and the technology which can help generate it. As society has developed rapidly, the amount of data and information being generated has increased exponentially. In order to handle the information, it is necessary to create the a system which has its own process. Even though there is a huge amount of data in our lives, most of the data and system is based in numbers and mathematical process. It helps that the process is controlled quickly and accurately. However, sometimes “digital” is can be seen as something cold or inaccessible in a way even though its algorithms make the our lives much easier.

A Shape of Emotions, it focuses on the emotional and subjective data rather than mathematical data. Man is a creature of impulse, and emotions are too comprehensive and complicated to be defined by number. There are hundreds of factors affecting our emotions, such as personality or personal circumstances. In this project, data is subjectively measured depending on feelings. I am designing a new system to take measurement of emotions by recording my own feelings every 15 minutes for a week. It is categorised by happiness, anger, sadness, confidence and appearance which shows how much I express my emotion and confidence. It is visualised in certain shapes, and created as a pattern which can be printed on fabrics or knitted with different colours.
